Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about finding a DOT physical near you.

Where can I get a DOT physical near me?

Doctors Place has over 15,000 partner exam locations across all 50 states. Go to portal.dot-physical.net and enter your zip code to find the nearest available location. Walk-ins are welcome at most sites, or you can book a specific time online.

Is there a DOT physical open today near me?

Yes — many locations offer same-day and next-day appointments. Book online to see real-time availability, or walk into any partner location during business hours. Most sites are open Monday through Friday, with some offering Saturday hours.

How much does a DOT physical cost near me?

DOT physicals through Doctors Place are $110 nationwide — the same price whether you're in New York, Texas, or California. Your FMCSA Medical Examiner Certificate is included at no extra charge.

Do I need an appointment for a DOT physical near me?

No appointment is required — walk-ins are welcome. However, booking online takes under 2 minutes and guarantees your time slot so you don't have to wait. You can book anytime at portal.dot-physical.net.

What do I need to bring to a DOT physical near me?

Bring a valid photo ID (driver's license or passport), your glasses or contacts if you use them for distance vision, and a list of any prescription medications. If you have a medical condition such as high blood pressure, sleep apnea, or diabetes, bring a note from your treating physician confirming it is controlled.

Can I get a DOT physical and drug test at the same location?

Yes — both services are available at most of our locations. DOT physical exams start at $110 and DOT drug screens (5-panel) start at $80. Many drivers complete both on the same visit.

How long does a DOT physical take?

Most DOT physicals take 30–45 minutes from check-in to certificate. If you book online and arrive with your documents ready, you can often be in and out in under 30 minutes.

Is the DOT physical result good nationwide?

Yes. Your FMCSA Medical Examiner Certificate is valid across all 50 states for up to 2 years (or 1 year if certain medical conditions are noted). The certificate is reported to the FMCSA National Registry automatically.
